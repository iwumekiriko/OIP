<p><b>Motoi Sakuraba</b><span style="font-weight: normal"> (<span title="Japanese-language text"><span lang="ja">桜庭 統</span></span>, <span title="Hepburn transliteration"><i lang="ja-Latn">Sakuraba Motoi</i></span>; born August 5, 1965)</span> is a Japanese composer and keyboardist. He is known for his numerous contributions in <a href="/wiki/Video_game" title="Video game">video games</a>, including the <i><a href="/wiki/Tales_(video_game_series)" title="Tales (video game series)">Tales</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Ocean" title="Star Ocean">Star Ocean</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Mario_Golf" class="mw-redirect" title="Mario Golf">Mario Golf</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Mario_Tennis" title="Mario Tennis">Mario Tennis</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Golden_Sun" title="Golden Sun">Golden Sun</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Dark_Souls" title="Dark Souls">Dark Souls</a></i> series, as well as several other anime series, television dramas, and <a href="/wiki/Progressive_rock" title="Progressive rock">progressive rock</a> albums.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Career">Career</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Motoi_Sakuraba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>Sakuraba was born on August 5, 1965, in <a href="/wiki/Akita_Prefecture" title="Akita Prefecture">Akita Prefecture</a>, Japan.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> While attending <a href="/wiki/Meiji_University" title="Meiji University">Meiji University</a>, Sakuraba began to take music composition seriously and formed the progressive rock band "Clashed Ice" in 1984, consisting of him on keyboards and Genta Kudo on drums and vocals.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-Bio&#95;4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bio-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> After the duo had graduated the following year, they were noticed by music producer Shingo Ueno, and ending up signing with Made in Japan Records. The band had then added bassist Tetsuya Nagatsuma, and was renamed as "Deja Vu".<sup id="cite&#95;ref-Bio&#95;4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bio-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 1988, the band would release their only studio album, <i>Baroque in the Future</i>, composed by Sakuraba.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-Bio&#95;4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bio-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Although the band disbanded in 1989, Sakuraba would later go on to release another solo album, <i><a href="/wiki/Gikyokuonsou" title="Gikyokuonsou">Gikyokuonsou</a></i>, in 1991.
</p><p>In late 1989, Sakuraba began working as a composer for <a href="/wiki/Wolf_Team" class="mw-redirect" title="Wolf Team">Wolf Team</a>, joining Masaaki Uno and Yasunori Shiono. The professional friendships formed here in Sakuraba's early years have resulted in a great demand for his composing and arranging abilities. In 1994, former Wolf Team director and composer Masaaki Uno started working at <a href="/wiki/Camelot_Software_Planning" title="Camelot Software Planning">Camelot Software Planning</a> as a coordinator and sound director, developing games for <a href="/wiki/Sony" title="Sony">Sony</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sega" title="Sega">Sega</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nintendo" title="Nintendo">Nintendo</a>. Sakuraba has been called upon as a composer for many Camelot games, including all of the games in the <i><a href="/wiki/Mario_Golf" class="mw-redirect" title="Mario Golf">Mario Golf</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Mario_Tennis" title="Mario Tennis">Mario Tennis</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Golden_Sun" title="Golden Sun">Golden Sun</a></i> series.
</p><p>In 1995, Wolf Team developed the breakthrough game <i><a href="/wiki/Tales_of_Phantasia" title="Tales of Phantasia">Tales of Phantasia</a></i> for <a href="/wiki/Namco" title="Namco">Namco</a>. This and other early games in the <a href="/wiki/Tales_(video_game_series)" title="Tales (video game series)"><i>Tales</i> series</a> primarily featured Sakuraba and fellow Wolf Team co-worker Shinji Tamura as composers. Also in 1995, former Wolf Team director and producer Jun Asanuma, as well as <i>Tales of Phantasia</i> writer and programmer <a href="/wiki/Yoshiharu_Gotanda" class="mw-redirect" title="Yoshiharu Gotanda">Yoshiharu Gotanda</a>, founded <a href="/wiki/Tri-Ace" title="Tri-Ace">tri-Ace</a> with financial backing from <a href="/wiki/Enix" title="Enix">Enix</a>. The <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Ocean" title="Star Ocean">Star Ocean</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Valkyrie_Profile" title="Valkyrie Profile">Valkyrie Profile</a></i> games have been their main franchises. Sakuraba has been the composer for nearly all of their games.
</p><p>In 1999, long time Sakuraba sound designer and programmer <a href="/wiki/Hiroya_Hatsushiba" title="Hiroya Hatsushiba">Hiroya Hatsushiba</a>, a former member of Wolf Team and tri-Ace, founded <a href="/wiki/Tri-Crescendo" title="Tri-Crescendo">tri-Crescendo</a>. While initially continuing to contribute sound work to tri-Ace games, tri-Crescendo began game development in 2001. Together with <a href="/wiki/Monolith_Soft" title="Monolith Soft">Monolith Soft</a>, tri-Crescendo started working on <i><a href="/wiki/Baten_Kaitos:_Eternal_Wings_and_the_Lost_Ocean" title="Baten Kaitos: Eternal Wings and the Lost Ocean">Baten Kaitos: Eternal Wings and the Lost Ocean</a></i>; Namco provided the financial backing. Hatsushiba, as director and main programmer of the project, again called upon Sakuraba's composing services. This has extended to the sequel, <i><a href="/wiki/Baten_Kaitos_Origins" title="Baten Kaitos Origins">Baten Kaitos Origins</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Eternal_Sonata" title="Eternal Sonata">Eternal Sonata</a></i>. The remnants of Wolf Team later morphed into the Namco Telenet joint subsidiary <a href="/wiki/Namco_Tales_Studio" title="Namco Tales Studio">Namco Tales Studio</a> in 2003.
</p><p>In 2007, Sakuraba was selected to join a long list of video game composers to arrange music for <i><a href="/wiki/Super_Smash_Bros._Brawl" title="Super Smash Bros. Brawl">Super Smash Bros. Brawl</a></i>. He chose to arrange the famous "Menu Theme" from the game's predecessor, <i><a href="/wiki/Super_Smash_Bros._Melee" title="Super Smash Bros. Melee">Super Smash Bros. Melee</a></i>. He also arranged "Gourmet Race" from <i><a href="/wiki/Kirby_Super_Star" title="Kirby Super Star">Kirby Super Star</a></i>, "Jungle Level Ver.2" from <i><a href="/wiki/Donkey_Kong_Country" title="Donkey Kong Country">Donkey Kong Country</a></i>, "Mario Tennis / Mario Golf" from <i><a href="/wiki/Mario_Golf:_Toadstool_Tour" title="Mario Golf: Toadstool Tour">Mario Golf: Toadstool Tour</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Mario_Power_Tennis" title="Mario Power Tennis">Mario Power Tennis</a></i>, "Victory Road" from <i><a href="/wiki/Pok%C3%A9mon_Ruby_and_Sapphire" title="Pokémon Ruby and Sapphire">Pokémon Ruby and Sapphire</a></i>, the "Airship Theme" from <i><a href="/wiki/Super_Mario_Bros._3" title="Super Mario Bros. 3">Super Mario Bros. 3</a></i>, the "Battlefield Theme", "Final Destination", and "Battle Scene / Final Boss (Golden Sun)" from <i><a href="/wiki/Golden_Sun:_The_Lost_Age" title="Golden Sun: The Lost Age">Golden Sun: The Lost Age</a></i>.
</p><p>Sakuraba then continued to write music for games in franchises he has worked with before, including <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Ocean:_The_Last_Hope" title="Star Ocean: The Last Hope">Star Ocean: The Last Hope</a></i>,<sup id="cite&#95;ref-VGMO&#95;Interview&#95;6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VGMO_Interview-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Golden_Sun:_Dark_Dawn" title="Golden Sun: Dark Dawn">Golden Sun: Dark Dawn</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Mario_Tennis_Open" title="Mario Tennis Open">Mario Tennis Open</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Mario_Golf:_World_Tour" title="Mario Golf: World Tour">Mario Golf: World Tour</a></i>, and many <a href="/wiki/Tales_(video_game_series)" title="Tales (video game series)"><i>Tales</i> series</a> games, including <i><a href="/wiki/Tales_of_Hearts" title="Tales of Hearts">Hearts</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Tales_of_Graces" title="Tales of Graces">Graces</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Tales_of_Xillia" title="Tales of Xillia">Xillia</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Tales_of_Xillia_2" title="Tales of Xillia 2">Xillia 2</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Tales_of_Zestiria" title="Tales of Zestiria">Zestiria</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Tales_of_Berseria" title="Tales of Berseria">Berseria</a></i>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-VGMO&#95;Interview&#95;6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VGMO_Interview-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> He has also contributed to other well known games such as the <i><a href="/wiki/Dark_Souls" title="Dark Souls">Dark Souls</a></i> series, <i><a href="/wiki/Kid_Icarus:_Uprising" title="Kid Icarus: Uprising">Kid Icarus: Uprising</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Phantasy_Star_Nova" title="Phantasy Star Nova">Phantasy Star Nova</a></i>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-VGMO&#95;Interview&#95;6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VGMO_Interview-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite&#95;ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 2014, he was selected to provide musical arrangements for Brawl's successor, <i><a href="/wiki/Super_Smash_Bros._for_Nintendo_3DS_and_Wii_U" title="Super Smash Bros. for Nintendo 3DS and Wii U">Super Smash Bros. for Nintendo 3DS and Wii U</a></i>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> For this title, Sakuraba arranged "Theme from Area 6 / Missile Slipstream" from <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Fox_64" title="Star Fox 64">Star Fox 64</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Fox_Command" title="Star Fox Command">Star Fox Command</a></i>, "Battle! (Team Flare)" from <i><a href="/wiki/Pok%C3%A9mon_X_and_Y" title="Pokémon X and Y">Pokémon X and Y</a></i>, and "The valedictory elegy" from <i>Baten Kaitos Origins</i>.
</p><p>In addition, Sakuraba has continued to write for non-gaming and anime projects, including solo albums such as "Forest of Glass", "What's Up?", and "Passage", as well as performing on several arrange albums.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-VGMO&#95;Interview&#95;6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VGMO_Interview-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In the 2020s, he composed for games such as <i><a href="/wiki/One_Piece_Odyssey" title="One Piece Odyssey">One Piece Odyssey</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Eiyuden_Chronicle:_Hundred_Heroes" title="Eiyuden Chronicle: Hundred Heroes">Eiyuden Chronicle: Hundred Heroes</a></i>.
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Live_performances">Live performances</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Motoi_Sakuraba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Live performances"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>During July 2003, Sakuraba held a live concert in Tokyo, Japan. He performed progressive rock interpretations of his music from games <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Ocean:_Till_the_End_of_Time" title="Star Ocean: Till the End of Time">Star Ocean: Till the End of Time</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Ocean:_The_Second_Story" title="Star Ocean: The Second Story">Star Ocean: The Second Story</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Ocean:_Blue_Sphere" title="Star Ocean: Blue Sphere">Star Ocean: Blue Sphere</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Valkyrie_Profile" title="Valkyrie Profile">Valkyrie Profile</a></i>. His bandmates for this concert were bassist Atsushi Hasegawa (a member of the band <a href="/wiki/Gerard_(band)" title="Gerard (band)">Gerard</a>) and drummer Toshihiko Nakamura.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> As noted, this concert was released on DVD and CD. At the same time as they were rehearsing for the concert, Hasegawa and Nakamura assisted in recording new material for the Director's Cut of <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Ocean:_Till_the_End_of_Time" title="Star Ocean: Till the End of Time">Star Ocean: Till the End of Time</a></i>.
</p><p>The following year, the trio performed another concert. The venue was smaller, but included music from <i>Baten Kaitos</i> and a couple of new, non-game-related pieces. This concert was not officially recorded and released, however. However, in 2006, a new concert was given in celebration of tri-Ace's <i><a href="/wiki/Valkyrie_Profile:_Silmeria" class="mw-redirect" title="Valkyrie Profile: Silmeria">Valkyrie Profile: Silmeria</a></i>.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>In September 2011, Sakuraba and his band joined up with the <a href="/wiki/Earthbound_Papas" title="Earthbound Papas">Earthbound Papas</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kawasaki,_Kanagawa" title="Kawasaki, Kanagawa">Kawasaki</a>, led by former <i><a href="/wiki/Final_Fantasy" title="Final Fantasy">Final Fantasy</a></i> series composer <a href="/wiki/Nobuo_Uematsu" title="Nobuo Uematsu">Nobuo Uematsu</a>, to perform at Fantasy Rock Fest 2011. The event featured game music performances, along with new <a href="/wiki/Progressive_rock" title="Progressive rock">progressive rock</a> pieces from both bands. The first six tracks that Sakuraba and his band played were sold at the event as a preview CD for his then-upcoming solo album <i>After all...</i>, which was released by Strange Days Records on October 19, 2011.
</p><p>In June 2015, Sakuraba performed at the <i>Tales of</i> Festival at the <a href="/wiki/Yokohama_Arena" title="Yokohama Arena">Yokohama Arena</a>, being the first time he performed tracks from the <i><a href="/wiki/Tales_(video_game_series)" title="Tales (video game series)">Tales</a></i> series at a live event.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> A selection of Sakuraba's music from the <i><a href="/wiki/Dark_Souls" title="Dark Souls">Dark Souls</a></i> and <i>Tales</i> series was performed live at the <a href="/wiki/Salle_Pleyel" title="Salle Pleyel">Salle Pleyel</a> concert hall in Paris in February 2017.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-Orchestral&#95;Memories&#95;12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Orchestral_Memories-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The event, known as "Orchestral Memories", featured a guest appearance by Sakuraba.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-Orchestral&#95;Memories&#95;12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Orchestral_Memories-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The 2020 <i>Tales of</i> Festival featured an original song by him called "Endless Journey", dedicated to the 25th anniversary of the series.<sup id="cite&#95;ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
